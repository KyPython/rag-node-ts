# Commercial Lease Agreement Requirements

## Essential Lease Terms

### Parties and Property
- Complete identification of lessor (landlord) and lessee (tenant)
- Detailed property description, including square footage, common areas, and parking rights
- Legal description and address of the leased premises
- Description of any shared facilities or common areas

### Lease Term and Rent

#### Lease Duration
- Commencement date and expiration date
- Renewal options and conditions
- Early termination provisions
- Holdover terms and penalties

#### Base Rent
- Monthly or annual base rent amount
- Rent payment schedule (due dates)
- Late payment fees and interest charges
- Rent escalation clauses (fixed increases, CPI adjustments, percentage rent)

#### Additional Charges
- Common area maintenance (CAM) charges
- Property taxes and insurance (triple net leases)
- Utilities and services
- Percentage rent (for retail leases)

### Use and Restrictions

#### Permitted Use
- Specific business activities allowed on premises
- Restrictions on use (exclusivity clauses, prohibited uses)
- Compliance with zoning and building codes
- Compliance with insurance requirements

#### Alterations and Improvements
- Tenant's right to make alterations
- Required landlord approval process
- Responsibility for costs and removal at lease end
- Ownership of improvements

### Maintenance and Repairs

#### Tenant Responsibilities
- Routine maintenance and repairs
- Compliance with laws and regulations
- Keeping premises in good condition
- Reporting required maintenance to landlord

#### Landlord Responsibilities
- Structural repairs and major systems
- Common area maintenance
- Compliance with building codes
- Insurance coverage requirements

### Insurance and Indemnification

#### Required Insurance
- General liability insurance ($1-2 million typically)
- Property insurance for tenant improvements
- Business interruption insurance
- Worker's compensation insurance

#### Indemnification
- Tenant indemnification of landlord
- Mutual indemnification for negligence
- Insurance requirements as prerequisites to indemnification

### Default and Remedies

#### Tenant Default
- Failure to pay rent
- Breach of lease terms
- Bankruptcy or insolvency
- Abandonment of premises

#### Landlord Remedies
- Right to terminate lease
- Right to re-enter and relet
- Recovery of damages
- Right to accelerate rent

#### Landlord Default
- Failure to maintain common areas
- Failure to provide services
- Breach of quiet enjoyment
- Tenant remedies (rent abatement, termination)

### Assignment and Subletting
- Tenant's right to assign or sublet (typically requires landlord consent)
- Landlord's right to withhold consent (reasonable vs. unreasonable)
- Conditions for assignment (creditworthiness, use restrictions)
- Original tenant's liability after assignment

### Security Deposits
- Amount of security deposit
- Conditions for return
- Use of deposit for damages
- Interest on deposit (varies by jurisdiction)

### Compliance and Environmental
- Compliance with Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Environmental compliance and liability
- Asbestos and lead-based paint disclosures
- Hazardous materials restrictions

### Dispute Resolution
- Mediation requirements
- Arbitration clauses
- Choice of law and venue
- Attorney's fees provisions

## Key Considerations

### For Tenants
1. Negotiate favorable renewal options
2. Understand all additional charges and how they're calculated
3. Ensure permitted use allows for business growth
4. Clarify maintenance responsibilities
5. Negotiate assignment rights

### For Landlords
1. Protect property value with use restrictions
2. Ensure adequate security deposit
3. Include strong default and remedy provisions
4. Clarify CAM charges and pass-through expenses
5. Limit tenant alterations and improvements

## Legal Requirements
- Must comply with local and state landlord-tenant laws
- Fair housing and anti-discrimination laws apply
- Consumer protection laws may apply to certain lease terms
- Professional legal review recommended for all commercial leases
