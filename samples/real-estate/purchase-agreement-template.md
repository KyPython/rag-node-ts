## Contingencies

### Inspection Contingency
Buyer has the right to conduct inspections of the property within [Number] days. If inspections reveal material defects, Buyer may:
1. Request repairs from Seller
2. Renegotiate the purchase price
3. Terminate the agreement and receive refund of earnest money

### Appraisal Contingency
If financing is required, the property must appraise for at least the Purchase Price. If the appraisal is below the Purchase Price, Buyer may terminate the agreement or negotiate a price reduction.

### Title Contingency
Seller must provide clear and marketable title, free of liens and encumbrances, except for those specifically agreed upon. Title insurance will be obtained at [Buyer's/Seller's] expense.

## Closing

### Closing Date
The closing shall occur on or before [Date], at [Location], or such other date and location as mutually agreed.

### Closing Costs
- Buyer typically pays: Loan origination fees, title insurance, inspection fees, recording fees, prepaid taxes and insurance
- Seller typically pays: Real estate commissions, transfer taxes, title insurance (in some states), property taxes prorated through closing date

### Prorations
Property taxes, homeowners association fees, and other recurring expenses will be prorated as of the closing date.

## Property Condition and Disclosures

### As-Is Clause
[If applicable] Property is sold "as-is" with no warranties except as specifically stated in this agreement.

### Seller Disclosures
Seller must provide:
- Property condition disclosure statement
- Lead-based paint disclosure (for properties built before 1978)
- Homeowners association documents (if applicable)
- Known defects and material facts

## Default and Remedies

### Buyer Default
If Buyer fails to close without a valid reason, Seller may:
- Retain earnest money as liquidated damages
- Seek specific performance
- Pursue other legal remedies

### Seller Default
If Seller fails to close or cannot deliver clear title, Buyer may:
- Receive refund of earnest money plus expenses
- Seek specific performance
- Pursue other legal remedies
